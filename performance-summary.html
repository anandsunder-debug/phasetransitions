Assessment

What This Means For The App
  • Customer experience during load is not acceptable yet. Median latency above 1.4s and a 17.4% failure rate are likely to damage conversion.
  • Auto-healing is functioning. It is enabled, learning, pruning exhausted actions, and still finding a few capacity-driven actions that produce positive SRI lift.
  • Auto-healing is not enough by itself. The system ends in an excellent live reliability state, but only after absorbing substantial request failures during the test.
  • Best current lever: prioritize scale-out actions tied to Cache and DB, because those are the most consistently positive interventions in the live data.

Recommended Next Actions

Performance and Healing Follow-up
  • Instrument the perf runner to store per-phase pass/fail and latency distributions in the JSON report so test-time degradation can be matched directly to healing activity.
  • Record healing snapshots during each ramp phase, not only after the run, so you can prove whether healing mitigates peak load or only improves post-load recovery.
  • Promote capacity-oriented actions earlier in the ladder for heavy ramp phases, especially cache and DB scale-out.
  • Investigate why high request failure persists while current reliability remains excellent; this usually indicates a mismatch between user-facing errors and back-end resilience scoring windows.
